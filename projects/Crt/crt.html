Goals

Crt Display

Old school CRT displays work in a very different way than modern LED displays. Instead of having a grid of pixels that are all drawn at the same time, CRT screens have a custom grid that is rendered one line after the other.

Many CRT displays dont even follow a standard grid system, and each have their own arrangement

What makes them even more odd is that they are much larger than LED pixels, and CRT "pixels" are just like LED pixels composed of sub-pixels, which in CRT displays oftem make then not even a square and with larger gaps between pixels.

How to Achieve it?

Pixels

I created a texture for a pixel, using RGB channels for the subpixels. They are sligthly overlapping to allow for the nice visual of a white color when all color channels are at use.

Creating a basic Grid

I am rendering a scene as a texture that i am simply multiplying with a grid of pixels, this already looks nice.
